## Continuing Education (CE) Requirements

### FREQUENCY AND HOURS

**Renewal Cycle:** Every 2 years

**Required Hours:** 24 hours every 2 years

**Breakdown:**
- 24 total hours required
- Must include **3 hours of Ethics/Moral and Regulatory Requirements**
- Remaining 21 hours can be any approved insurance topics

### Memory Trick
**24 hours every 2 years** | **3 of those must be Ethics**

### CE Exemptions

**For Credit Insurance Only:**
- If licensed ONLY for Credit Insurance
- You may have different requirements
- Still must complete Ethics hours
- Check current Michigan DIFS regulations for specifics

### Timing

**Renewal:** Licenses expire at specific dates
**CE must be completed:** Before license expiration
**Failure to complete:** License lapses automatically

## Fiduciary Responsibility

**Fiduciary = Acting in someone else's best interest**

### What It Means

As an insurance producer, you have a legal duty to:

**1. Act in the best interests of customers**
- Not just what makes you the most money
- Recommend what's best for them
- Even if it means lower commission for you

**2. Provide accurate information**
- Don't lie or mislead
- Disclose important facts
- Answer questions honestly

**3. Not deceive or misrepresent**
- Can't hide bad terms in fine print
- Can't exaggerate coverage
- Can't make false promises

**4. Disclose conflicts of interest**
- If you benefit from a recommendation, say so
- If you have a personal relationship, disclose it
- Transparency is key

**5. To all parties EXCEPT the Commissioner**
- You owe fiduciary duty to customer
- You owe fiduciary duty to company
- You do NOT owe fiduciary duty to DIFS
- (DIFS is the regulator; fiduciary applies to business relationships)

### Why It Matters

- Protects customers from being exploited
- Makes insurance market fair and honest
- Establishes trust
- Required by law

## Commission Sharing and Referral Fees

### COMMISSION SHARING

**What it means:**
- You receive a commission, then share part of it with another person

**Who can you share with?**
- **ONLY with another agent licensed in the same line of insurance**

**Examples of ALLOWED commission sharing:**
- You and another agent co-sell a policy → can split commission
- You refer to another licensed agent → can negotiate commission split
- Team of agents → can share commissions

**Examples of NOT ALLOWED:**
- Sharing commission with unlicensed person (illegal)
- Sharing with customer (would be rebating)
- Sharing with non-insurance professional

### REFERRAL FEES

**What they are:**
- Money paid for referring a customer to someone else
- May or may not result in a sale

**Key Rule: Contingency**

**ALLOWED: Non-contingent referral fees**
- "Refer someone and get $50 regardless of whether they buy"
- Fee is not contingent (dependent) on a sale
- Legal in Michigan

**NOT ALLOWED: Contingent referral fees**
- "Refer someone and get $50 IF they buy"
- Fee is contingent on the purchase
- This is considered rebating
- Illegal

### Memory Trick
**Contingent = bad (tied to sale)** | **Non-contingent = good (paid regardless)**

---

## PROCESSING FEES

**What they are:**
- Fees charged for processing an insurance transaction
- Example: Credit card processing fee

**The Rule:**
- **NOT allowed for credit card payments**
- Cannot charge customer extra to pay by credit card
- Credit card companies already charge processing fees
- Cannot double-charge

**Why?**
- Protects consumers from excessive fees
- Prevents abuse of credit card processing

### Examples

**NOT ALLOWED:**
- "Processing fee: $25 if paying by credit card"
- "$50 surcharge for credit card payments"
- "Credit card convenience fee"

**ALLOWED:**
- Charging different prices for different payment methods (generally)
- Building credit card fees into the premium itself

## CHANGE OF ADDRESS

### Notification Requirement

**When you move:**
- Must notify DIFS within **30 days**
- Provide new address
- Keep information current

**Why it matters:**
- DIFS needs to reach you for notices
- License renewal communications
- Disciplinary matters
- Legal documents

**Failure to notify:**
- May result in penalty
- Could affect license status
- DIFS can't contact you

## ETHICAL OBLIGATIONS SUMMARY

| Obligation | Requirement | Example |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| **Best Interest** | Act in customer's best interest | Recommend best policy, not highest commission |
| **Accuracy** | Provide accurate information | Don't exaggerate coverage |
| **No Deception** | Don't mislead or misrepresent | Don't hide bad terms |
| **Disclose Conflicts** | Tell customer about conflicts | Disclose if you profit from recommendation |
| **Share Only with Licensed Agents** | Commission sharing restricted | Can't share with unlicensed person |
| **Non-contingent Fees Only** | Referral fees must not depend on sale | "Refer for $50 regardless" = OK |
| **No CC Processing Fees** | Can't charge for credit card use | Can't add surcharge |
| **Keep Address Current** | Notify DIFS within 30 days | Must update if you move |

## Key Definitions

- **Fiduciary:** Acting in someone else's best interest
- **Contingent:** Dependent on something happening
- **Non-contingent:** Not dependent on something happening
- **Rebating:** Offering something of value to induce a sale (illegal)
- **Referral Fee:** Payment for referring customer
- **Commission:** Payment for selling insurance
